# Weekly Review Gotchas

Common failures that make reviews ineffective:

## Review Killers

### 1. Perfectionism Paralysis
- ❌ "I didn't accomplish anything meaningful this week"
- ❌ Focusing only on what went wrong or incomplete
- ✅ Find at least one small win, even if the week was rough

### 2. Task List Confusion
- ❌ Using review time just to list next week's to-dos
- ❌ Getting lost in tactical details instead of patterns
- ✅ Focus on themes and energy allocation, not task management

### 3. Historical Dwelling
- ❌ Spending 80% of review time relitigating past decisions
- ❌ Turning reflection into rumination
- ✅ Quick backward glance, longer forward focus

### 4. Metrics Obsession
- ❌ Tracking 15 different metrics that don't connect to decisions
- ❌ Feeling bad about numbers without understanding context
- ✅ Track 2-3 things that actually inform how you plan your week

### 5. Comparison Trap
- ❌ "Everyone else seems more productive/focused/successful"  
- ❌ Using review to beat yourself up
- ✅ Compare this week to last week, not to others

### 6. Review Creep
- ❌ Turning 15-minute reflection into 2-hour analysis session
- ❌ Making it so complex you skip it
- ✅ Set timer, stick to format, keep it sustainable

## Effectiveness Check

Good weekly reviews are:
- **Forward-weighted** (more time on next week than last)
- **Pattern-focused** (trends over events)  
- **Actionable** (influences real decisions)
- **Sustainable** (you actually do them consistently)
