# Research Methodology

## 6-Step Research Process

### 1. Source Discovery
- Use multiple search strategies (academic, news, industry, forums)
- Start broad, then narrow based on initial findings
- Aim for 5-8 potential sources before filtering

### 2. Source Evaluation  
Apply credibility filters:
- **Authority**: Author credentials and institutional affiliation
- **Accuracy**: Fact-checking, peer review, editorial standards
- **Objectivity**: Potential bias, funding sources, conflicts of interest
- **Currency**: Publication date and information freshness
- **Coverage**: Scope and depth of treatment

### 3. Information Extraction
For each source:
- Identify key claims and supporting evidence
- Note methodology used (if research study)
- Extract relevant quotes with page numbers/links
- Flag areas of uncertainty or limitation

### 4. Cross-Verification
- Look for consensus across independent sources
- Identify contradictions and investigate causes
- Check if single-source claims can be verified elsewhere
- Note where sources cite each other (avoid echo chambers)

### 5. Synthesis
- Organize findings by theme/question
- Weight evidence based on source quality
- Assign confidence levels: High (3+ quality sources agree), Medium (2 sources or qualified agreement), Low (single source or conflicting evidence)
- Identify knowledge gaps

### 6. Documentation
- Cite all sources with accessible links
- Include search terms and databases used
- Note limitations and areas for future research
- Provide confidence rating for overall analysis
