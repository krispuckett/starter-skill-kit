# Source Quality Evaluation

## Tier 1: High Authority
- Peer-reviewed academic journals
- Government statistical agencies  
- Established research institutions
- Primary research from reputable organizations
- Original documentation/datasets

## Tier 2: Reliable
- Established news organizations with editorial standards
- Industry trade publications
- Professional associations
- Think tanks (with disclosed funding)
- Expert interviews/quotes

## Tier 3: Supplementary  
- Wikipedia (as starting point, not final source)
- Blog posts from recognized experts
- Company white papers (with bias awareness)
- Crowdsourced data (with sample size context)
- Social media (for trends, not facts)

## Red Flags
- No author attribution
- No publication date
- Obvious commercial bias without disclosure
- Extraordinary claims without extraordinary evidence
- Circular citations (A cites B, B cites A)
- Anonymous sources for factual claims

## Verification Checklist

**Author Credentials:**
- [ ] Relevant expertise in the subject area
- [ ] Institutional affiliation listed
- [ ] Other work in the field findable

**Publication Standards:**
- [ ] Editorial review process evident
- [ ] Fact-checking standards disclosed
- [ ] Corrections/retractions policy visible

**Transparency:**
- [ ] Funding sources disclosed
- [ ] Methodology explained (for research)
- [ ] Limitations acknowledged
- [ ] Conflicts of interest stated

**Currency:**
- [ ] Publication date recent enough for topic
- [ ] Information hasn't been superseded
- [ ] References to current, not outdated, understanding
